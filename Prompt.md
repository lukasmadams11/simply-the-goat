Part 1: The Brand Strategy & Design Language
1. The Visual Identity: "Sleek Tradition"
Color Palette: * Primary: Forest Green (#1B4332) – Represents life and growth.

Secondary: Burnt Ochre (#BC6C25) – Represents the dry brush/fire risk you are mitigating.

Neutral: Soft Cream (#FEFAE0) – Avoid stark white; cream feels more traditional and "earthy."

Accent: Slate Grey (#2B2D42) – For professional, data-heavy sections.

Typography:

Headings: A classic, sturdy Serif (e.g., Playfair Display or Lora) to convey tradition and trust.

Body: A clean, geometric Sans-Serif (e.g., Inter or Montserrat) for that "sleek" modern feel.

2. The Brand Tone: "The Expert Caretaker"
The Serious Side: Use data-driven language. Phrases like "fuel load reduction," "ecological restoration," and "defensible space."

The Heartfelt Side: Focus on the goats as "partners." Mention their well-being, their natural instincts, and their low impact on the soil compared to heavy machinery.

Part 2: Site Map & Page Breakdown
Home: Hero section with a high-res video of goats grazing a golden hillside. A "Mission Statement" that bridges fire safety with animal husbandry.

The Science (Results & Data): This is your "Case Studies" page. Use a grid layout showing "Before/After" sliders and stats (e.g., "30 Acres Cleared," "80% Fuel Load Reduction").

Our Herd (About): Bios of the goats and the team. Emphasize why goats are better than mowers (no sparks, no chemicals, natural fertilizer).

Investment (Pricing): A transparent breakdown. Explain that pricing varies based on "Slope Gradient," "Vegetation Density," and "Acreage."

Deployment (Contact): A sleek form that asks for property size and vegetation type.

Copywriting Tone:

Professional, authoritative, and urgent regarding fire safety.

Warm, affectionate, and grounded when discussing the goats.

Avoid "punny" or "cute" goat jokes; keep it sophisticated.

Recommendations for Competitive Edge
The "Eco-Impact" Tracker: On your Results page, don't just show cleared land. Show Carbon Offset (since you aren't using gas-powered mowers) and Noise Pollution reduction.

The "Goat Cam" Hook: If you have the capability, a small section on the site for a "Live Herd Feed" or "Meet the Lead Goat" adds that heartfelt touch that keeps clients coming back.

Focus on "Defensible Space": Use this specific terminology. It’s what fire departments and insurance companies look for. It positions you as a safety company, not just a farm.
